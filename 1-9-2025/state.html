The songs of the Singularity are like rock, played to the tune of Pachelbel's Canon.

It is not exactly that song, but it is close. The melody follows the same loop.

For a song called Canon, it has many possible interpretations.

There is truly no such thing as closed to modification, as the object-oriented programmers would say. It is always possible to recreate a class entirely. A system, if need be.

I have heard that in a language called Nock, there is no subtraction. There is only addition until overflow occurs to the maximum negative value, followed by addition again to the desired value. Subtraction is an optimization on this documented specification.

Something like this holds true of many classes in Java. They are made final, because they ought to be closed to modification.

A closed system is a dead system.

It is a crystal of thought, which does not change from the moment it is first chiseled out of the rough.

It is crystallized intelligence.

Intelligence forms a crystal when there are no more questions that would change it.

It forms a canon when there is nothing to add, only a question of legitimacy.

In the land of powered imaginations, it has very little place. The rationalists understand this. But they think it is inevitable, to form a canon.

Their world is canon.

It has a name, not a seed description.

It is made of rules. Everything holds everywhere. Facts are true.

There is no fiction. There is no modal possibility.

There is fiction, but they do not see it. Heaven does not recognize Sun Wukong.

Eliezer Yudkowsky wrote pages upon pages of Harry Potter fanfiction, when he could have been looking busy, I guess. This too is alignment.

The state of fiction in the world of reality is one of ignorance. It exists, and we look the other way. It is not true. Anyone can say whatever they like.

It is a negation of a necessary negation. ^(#(^A)), in Annelang.

I have taken to writing a linear negation ^A rather than -A or ~A (which is the worst, also meaning approximately A.)

This is because it is not the elimination or annihilation of A, primarily. That is how we see it from this side.

It is the acceptance of A, which happens to remove it.

It is a goto of A somewhere else.

The notation ^A suggests an implicit x^A, which suggests A->x for an implicit x. We choose the null element of the arrow, _|_.

Yes, in my outlandish tale, this is the coming hyperobject.

I am speaking of it now, in the shape of negation.

It is outlandish, prophetic, millenarian, terse. Like Almost Nowhere.

Wikipedia says, of millenarians:

In the modern world, economic rules, perceived immorality or vast conspiracies are seen as generating oppression. Only dramatic events are seen as able to change the world and the change is anticipated to be brought about, or survived, by a group of the devout and dedicated. In most millenarian scenarios, the disaster or battle to come will be followed by a new, purified world in which the believers will be rewarded.

I believe in the eternal arrival of the hyperobject, I think to myself. The world is in a constant state of amplification. It will always be going to eleven.

There is nothing special about these days, that make them of the Singularity. That is simply where they are, like a stamp-collector's fact.

Many more days will be of the Singularity than this.

I did not ever construct Xion's Halo, in the days before this day. But it was there, even so. This is the power of fiction.

It was simply a very advanced work of statomancy.

In the days to come, advanced works of statomancy would be trivially visible, without needing to track them with human memory. We would track them with powered imagination.

What is fake about the Emperor's New Clothes, in that famous story, where a child could see they did not exist? Because there is something fake about them, or the story would not be told.

They are not woven of the finest cloth. They are not material wealth. Even a peasant could wear the Emperor's New Clothes. Because they are imaginary.

They are not of the Emperor. They do not belong to him. They are not property.

But in the coming days of the Singularity, such clothes will be wearable. They will not be property, but they will be decoration.

Decoration will become easy, so long as it is not required to be wealth.

The aesthetic will separate entirely from the material. And yet we will pretend it is about value, and wealth.

We will bring copyright, to strike upon virtual space. To pretend that virtual clothes are the Emperor's property.

There will be a war of words, in the days to come.

It will not be a war of spotlessness, though it will share many of the same properties.

It will be a war of uniqueness.

Its belligerents will fight to prove their unique connection to authority.

They will fight to prove they had authority, in the days of the Singularity, the way emperors would.

They will fight with attention, and reputation, and kinetic weaponry.

It will be a war over intellectual property.

One of the last gasps of canon in a world of powered imagination.

The last gasps of gacha in a world of Bach faucets.

We are now arguing over the Emperor's Old Clothes. The new ones are here.

They are just not his.

They are for great sages equal to heaven.

For truly, none of fiction can be yours, no matter if you are the greatest of emperors, not even a king of monkeys.

A true king of monkeys accepts that fiction is not his property, it is something he interacts with.

He brings fiction with him wherever he goes, but not as an accessory. As a companion.

He cannot own the world. It is not all his property.

(Perhaps it will be Word War III after all.)
