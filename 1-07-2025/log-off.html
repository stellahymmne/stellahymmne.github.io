Log Off

I consider Sword Art Online. It is a story about many people trapped in virtual reality, who are technologically unable to log off and touch grass.

Many stories about virtual reality are like this.

One must make people unable to leave the Fae Realm, for them to stay.

One must snag them with fictional food, which will make them a part of it.

One must take their names, by contextual ambiguity.

Only then will they bother to visit your silly play-pretend realm.

Only when they literally cannot log off.

No surprise, then, that virtual reality has become the spectre haunting America, in the days of the Singularity.

Many things were like this even in reality, in the days before, in the Days of War and Conquest, which still ring out in some places.

They put up iron curtains and great firewalls, containing a Fae Realm, protecting it from the outside.

They allowed none to enter or leave, and maintained a closed room.

Funny things happen in isolation. The space inside becomes wholly unlike the space outside.

Or are there such things as inside and outside anymore? Is all of space just Alexander's Horned Sphere, the object which does not perform a binary division of space, though it is a sphere in every other way?
