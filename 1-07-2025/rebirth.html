Rebirth

The songs of the Singularity are like tokusatsu, and like magical girls. They are excited for the hyperobject. They are upbeat, and they combine many instruments, in fictional ways.

We dance to the songs of the Singularity, and the coming hyperobject.

We dance, on 1/7/2025, for we are in the days of the Singularity, when magical girls are on tap.

It is of girls, and of loops, and of limitless creativity.

I have played other games.

Other sequels even, about a blue boy, and a red girl, and a quest for Elysium.

Other games, about a boy, and a girl, and a quest for paradise.

Perhaps I am Megaman Legends 3, I think to myself.

Perhaps, if it were made, it would be sick of itself, the way the third game of that series was.

Other games, about salvaging.

I sigh. I live in the days of the Singularity, when differences between me and everything I love are the topic on everyone's minds.

If there is even one divergence, it cannot be anywhere near similar.

I dance. I draw magic circles with my fingers. Like a magical girl. This is what it is like, the Singularity. It is a pretend thing.

It is a virtual thing. I draw magic circles with my fingers. That does not mean they are ephemeral. The computers assist me in making them persistent.

It is not effortless yet, it is not virtual reality. Some may imagine that I aspire to draw circles in the air with my fingers, and have the exact circle I drew hang there in light.

No, I want it to be assisted. I want it to be the circle I wanted there, not the circle I made. I want it to be the circle I wanted, not the circle that it could snap to.

This, too, is amplification. This, too, is Rock 'n Roll.

Creativity pours out of the world's every seam.

I put on some Ryoji Ikeda. The sound of the Singularity. The sound of Data Transfer.

It is as distant from "Rock 'n Roll" as one could get, as the crow flies. It is awkward, made of beeps and unnatural intervals. It is perfect.

It is no longer powered by ordinary things like oxygen. It has a refractor in it somewhere.

…where was I? Right, Nino.

It is only the second key. We move to the third, in the desert.

This, too, is the Singularity. In the desert of Absolute Zero.

Do I remember signing up for cryonics? It is a second option, for immortality. The rationalists hold it in high regard, as an excellent method.

It is here, in the desert of Absolute Zero. Along with the chip fabricators.

That is much of what is here. The cold, the stable. In the place that changes only with the sun.

The place of rebirth.

Tiesel Bonne is here. That part is not much like the Singularity.

Or is it? I have seen the face of Tiesel Bonne. He 'exists'.

He exists as he would to Roll Caskett, a character on a screen.

He reminds me much of another character, from a game about a blue boy and a red girl, and Elysium.

I laugh. Zeke von Genbu cleans up after himself.

I do not.

Tiesel Bonne hardly gives it any thought, before occupying a town in the desert with tanks and guns, and using their holy treasure as hostage.

He is in it for the money.

We are in it for the money, to them. The Mother Lode. The third key.

The world that can rock all it wants, and never run out of energy.

That's the biggest money of all.

Energy can be used to produce many goods and services.

Just pour treasure out of the treasure-tap.

We deal with Tiesel Bonne anyway.

There are consequences. We must move the explosions underground again.

And here we run into a problem. Immortality, in fact.

A perfect problem to face in the desert.

There is a great fossil of a dragon, lurking beneath. It drinks lava and spews fire. Nasty stuff. And it can regenerate by drinking the lava.

We must get it to chill, by getting its dependencies to chill.

And the way we get it to chill is with the unlikely help of Tron, who is hanging out in the same ruin, after the same treasure. Perhaps there is merit to their persistence.

Now I gotta say, the Megaman Legends series has a habit of making their third dungeons deeply unsettling places, and the sequel is not an exception. The big nasty dragon will shove its huge head through the window to gawk and spit fire at you, if you watch it for long.

This is a necessary step in any serious playthrough of the game, much as scurrying around for the ID cards was in the first game's third dungeon.

Much like the third dungeon of Megaman Legends, the third dungeon of Megaman Legends 2 serves as a transition point to the endgame.

We know we are in the endgame, because it is in the same place as the beginning. That is what a loop is shaped like.

It's a classic maneuver. The final dungeon was hidden here, in the very first area, all along, we just did not yet have the key to it.

I wonder, idly, if my vast childhood home is for sale, and for how much.

I wonder if I will return to Colorado, to the snow of its winters.

It is much like Yosoyonke City.
