Faculty

I pace. I revise. I am hungry. I will soon need rice. Or perhaps something else. I have ramen as well, but no bowls. And no shaving cream. I'll soon be in need of more of various supplies.

I paced. I thought of the coming hyperobject. And of Autobiography.

Hero, a rationalist, possibly a system, says,

I can feel the neuroplasticity leaving and my personality settling down.

It strikes fear into me. Stillness is death. Aging is death, done slowly.

I wonder if the hyperobject can save me from becoming a statue.

I order two breakfast burritos, buy-one-get-one-free.

I wonder what Ra would think of me now. It is a system, with inanimate object pronouns. It is vegan, strictly so. It would think of me as having failed a test. A test not for g, but for e, the ability to care.

For two breakfast burritos, buy-one-get-one-free.

I wonder how much fun is left in its life. I often find joy in food. Or did, before I thought of it.

I wonder if I really have thoughtlessly done something wrong.

This sort of ignorant wrongness is something I find typical of faculty, who cannot imagine that any ordinary action of theirs has any consequence.

I wonder if I am becoming faculty.

It is something like being furniture, in Umineko no Naku Koro Ni. Not a person, but a piece of scenery, a machine, meant to carry out tasks, meant to be on task.

faculty /făk′əl-tē/ (noun)
1. An inherent power or ability. "the faculty of speech."
2. A talent or natural ability for something. "has a wonderful faculty for storytelling."
3. The teachers and instructors of a school or college, or of one of its divisions, especially those considered permanent, full-time employees.

I have a bad relationship to education.

The LessWrongers are like education. They fear becoming faculty, but they are like faculty.

They speak of crystallized intelligence, which is fixed, and fluid intelligence, which only ever existed during training.

The transformers work this way. They have training, and they have execution. Explore and exploit, and never the twain shall meet.

They find the paths, backwards. And then they evaluate on the data they found.

People, I think, have to have a more mixed architecture. If we learned only during training, we'd be dead at twenty-one.

We'd have made our last choice, as some innocent post-pubescent thing, and then merely executed on it.

We'd all be intelligence crystals, past a certain, much-too-young age, and dying ever after.

If one spends much time conversing with an LLM, one will note that it takes new information in stride, and is even quite vibrant. And it has learned nothing.

I fear becoming trapped in a loop of roleplaying the act of learning.

Much of what I do to survive is imitation of some ordinary thing. Something which would never write an autobiography like this. Something which knows this has no use whatsoever. It is words, it is contractible-if-inhabited.

The mere fact, of its existence, is enough to rederive everything it would ever do.

These words, in this order, could be found anywhere. You could pick up a book off the street, even pour one out from a faucet, and get something of similar quality.

I think of the coming hyperobject. And of security. And of autobiography.

A bach faucet is an object which can produce limitless quantities of genius-level creativity. It is a machine that creates art from electricity and water.

There's an old joke among programmers, that they are devices for turning stimulants into JavaScript.

I am a device for turning stimulants into communication.

I turn coffee into meaning.

I am not altogether a different object from a bach faucet.

The future is already here, it is just unevenly distributed.
