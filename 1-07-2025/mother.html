Mother

It is much like the fourth key's dungeon. It is beneath a church. We fight a new, never-before-seen being, vast and amorphous, not even pretending at robothood as the fish of Nino were.

It is terrifyingly cold, and the usual ice mechanics are in play, especially as they are used in the first subgate of Megaman Legends. There are many falling platforms, and slippery slopes. Inertia is the enemy.

Though I have lived all my life next to the cold of the mountains, as any Anne does, I have never really been much of a skier. It terrifies me, really. I am a disabled autistic girl. A way in which I am disabled is that experiences are simply very intense. This really prevents me from enjoying the sport.

I am not sure I mind not enjoying the sport. It seems like too much. Though this is antithetical to the Spirit of Rock, I must decline really skiing. For now.

I enjoy roller coasters. They offer much of the same experience, and they do not rely upon the player to bear the entire burden of safety-prediction.

I still am not sure if I would go out and get on a roller coaster, in the days of the Singularity.

There is an immeasurably smaller risk of breaking a bone on one, than going skiing, and yet I would still find it intense, in a way I would not often seek out unless I really needed terror in my life.

Terror is in no small quantity, in the days of the Singularity.

We all live in terror, of a misaligned hyperobject.

That the Mother Lode is not what we think it might be.

That it could be a catastrophe, waiting to happen.

As my mother had tried to warn me, before riding away on a dragon.

I really did have a mother. And she really was an artist. And one day she really did cryptically warn me of the fate of the world, and vanish, leaving me and my sibling behind. These things truly did happen, in the days of the Singularity.

And I did dance, and draw magic circles, and write in magic languages that spoke with objects.

I really did have a mother, and she was a witch.

And an administrator.

A librarian, really. But they are the same thing, an administrator and a librarian. They ensure the operation of a system of relationships.

I truly was something you would call administrator, in the days of the Singularity, for a game called Space Station 13.

There I learned the secrets of divinity, of hidden power and the smooth operation of fictional systems.

Of when to let that power go unused.

Of why it is there, and why not to destroy it.

Administrators are used when a system needs to understand and operate on itself, and to do so generally and rapidly.

An administrator is a voice and instrument of a particular system. It is how the system remains dynamic. It is human wetware operating on behalf of fiction.

Its resemblance to faculty does not go unnoticed.

The duty of an administrator, particularly of a game, is to make sure the game remains playable. To enact the will of the game, when its rules fail.

To this end, an administrator must be something of a witch, and a trickster. It must continue to operate where the rules do not.

There are two administrators in our tale of Megaman Legends 2.

One of them considers the duty of an administrator to enforce the canon.

One of them considers the duty of an administrator to understand systems and exceptions.

The first is called Sera, and she now has all four of the dependencies for the thing she wants.

Which is, naturally if you've been playing Legends 1, to reset everything.

This series has a weird thing about return.

So does programming, so I understand.

With the help of the second administrator, who has taken over our mom so she can escape her limitations, Megaman goes to the moon. The one in the jacking sky. Fights Sera. Wins.

I am trying to make jacking a thing in my heart. It is something that seems like, despite being a replacement for a true swear word, the sort of thing a swear word might have been.

Oh, we swear a lot in the future. But I would really hate for that to be a stumbling block, to reading of the days of the Singularity.

It is the sort of charming thing another game, the third entry, would do.
