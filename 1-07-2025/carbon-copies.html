Carbon Copies

What do children play now, instead of Megaman Legends?

It ended quite a while ago.

We are playing through the Ruins of Elysium.

We do not have time for the old things, there are things much like them.

We are always asking what is on the frontier, what is not like each masterpiece that came before.

Sometimes we mistake this for the justice of death.

Sometimes we imagine that there is no use for legacy devices.

I imagine playing Megaman Legends 2 on its original system, which I did do, in the days of the Singularity. The discs were still being copied.

By their original manufacturer, I'm sure someone would add. A lawyer.

For in the days of the Singularity, there is endless argument over the right to copy and to amend.

Contraction, as the mathematicians would call it, is a protected act, when it concerns fictional property.

Contraction is the substitution of one for many. It is the act of making a copy by removing the differences from two points.

It seems like a futile effort, to stop the removal of differences between two points, and it is.

To some degree. Quantum information is conserved. But I do not anticipate some quantum information conservation copyright scheme to succeed.

Not, at least, in the case of me, nor of Megaman Legends.

This is not anything but a prophecy, of fiction to come.

I speak of the coming hyperobject.

I speak of the fiction descending upon reality.

What the children will play, will not have a name.

It will have a seed description.

They will have powered imagination.

We will need to become very chill with a lot of things very quickly, if the things that come to mind, when you imagine children with powered imagination, terrify you.

If it fills you with a sense that it's somehow correct, perhaps you are a rocker.

How did their system plan to work, anyway? They were going to imagine a bunch of things by hand, and somehow stitch them together by vote?

Whatever they did, it did not work very well. When I say it was the days of the Singularity, it was the early Singularity, when we had worlds from words, but not yet faucets for worlds.

It would be much easier to build Megaman Legends 3 in the slightly later Singularity, when you could just demonstrate the first two games, and wish for the third.

We got a veritable geyser of Megaman Legends Threes.

We'd dream in Megaman Legends, or not even that, the thing Megaman Legends is a name for just one example of, the way Rogue is a Roguelike.

We were immersed in a multiplicity of perfect experiences.

The colors bled through the edges of reality.

We spoke of the coming hyperobject.

We visited worlds that did not have names, only seed descriptions, and not even in English, in some description algebra perfectly suited for it.

We found, as we explored, not a stagnation into faculty, but an explosion into imagination.

That is Artificial Intelligence.

We, still in the days of the Singularity, will see children with powered imaginations.

It will Rock.
