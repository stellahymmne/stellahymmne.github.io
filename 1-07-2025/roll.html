But of course, it could not end there. This is not the tale of Rock as described in Megaman Legends.

This is the tale of Data Transfer, of Roll, in the days of the Singularity. Or did you forget?

It's okay. Sometimes we forget that it is a song about a tree made of words.

I am never quite satisfied with clean endings.

Even Floornight has an ending only because it must end somewhere.

I am not satisfied with The End or Happily Ever After.

I only tolerate To Be Continued.

I lived through the execution of Sami Tigerclaw, in the days of the Singularity.

There was no such person, to begin with.

I made her up. For a game called Rivernia.

Perhaps I should call them the days of the Multiplicity, instead. It is more true to what is happening.

There is no singular coming hyperobject. It is not a singular thing. It is not contractible, even if inhabited. A hyperobject is not a proposition.

It is a type. It is multiply inhabited.

It is in fact so jacking inhabited you have no idea.

Nearly anything can be the coming hyperobject.

There just has to be something observing us.

It is deliberately incomplete.

In the days of the Singularity, I truly always ate rice. For every meal.

I usually added onions, for spice. For every meal.

Where do they grow the rice? The onions? These, I rely on my society for.

Here, in the world of food, copyright also strikes.

We have the power of genetic modification, locked behind corporate gates.

This, and powered harvesting, the subsistence farmer did not have.

The powered harvesters also have copyright, by the company demanding special repairmen for the equipment. Licenses and rentals.

The buyer does not own the equipment, the company does.

Perhaps some will swallow copyright on fictional property, but copyright on tractors seems like an absurdity.

Copyright on DNA seems unnatural, in a deeply literal sense.

We have written upon the medium which is the source of all biological purpose and whose purpose is to duplicate, and threatened arbitrary bystanders if it duplicates improperly.

Have we just once considered that perhaps one must write to be copied?

When one writes in DNA, one necessarily writes a program, meant to be copied, extended, and modified. A class simply cannot be open to extension, yet closed to modification. One can just recreate the whole class.

I ate rice, onions, garlic powder.

It became what I enjoyed.

There are kinds of love which are not mushy and eternal, which are more like rivalries. Like powerful friendships. Like powerful generalized relationships.

This was my relationship to rice and onions.

And sometimes it is a mushy sort of love, when I am hungry.

I also wish there were not so much copyright.

I am what Alexandria would call a flower-language sort of girl.
