Coffee

I wake up. It is a cold morning in Absolute Zero. I throw on a jacket. It's a nice jacket, at least.

The digital displays on the microwave and stove both read 0:00, because they are both timers which are at their end, or beginning.

Coffee.

I make it, my way, not my sibling's. Enough scoops of the cheap stuff to cover the base of a filter evenly, then six cups of water.

Some of them had called me a genius. I knew I was not. Communication is not a property one has. It is a property one does. It is a process. It is bilateral. I can't be a genius for the same reason the north pole of a magnet cannot. Or should it be the south?

But it is useful to pretend at being a genius. Geniuses are loved. When in reality, I made coffee much worse than a professional. You'd imagine that a genius, after seeing them do a pour over, would adopt the perfect technique at once, and then improve on it.

I remember their voice speaking of grinds, and over-steeping.

God's Gift to Grinds, like Dave Strider. A different kind of grind, but I love puns.

The coffee machine bubbles in the background. Cleaning an empty mug with some water and the last of the paper towels. I ignore the rest of the mess. I can't do anything but punish myself about it. Or like, dedicate so much time to it I'd burst and cry. That'd be the default solution. To ignore me and become what is needed. Right now.

But I am not a parent. I am a person, an ordinary one. One who does not become a domestic superhero, no matter how much saving the day is required. I think of the coming hyperobject. I wonder how long it will be before we can automate cleaning.

We already have the Roomba, but this place is the anti-Roomba. It is full of chairs and rugs. It is all hardwood floors. I have made a fine layer of rice particles everywhere I've been. Sometimes I pick up a broom and pathetically sweep some up, only for it to return before I am finished with the rest. I think of all the wonderful food it is possible to eat at breakfast. Toast, scrambled eggs, hash browns. I no longer think of bacon, it is too cruel. At least eggs can theoretically be obtained better.

I wonder how long it will be before meat is lab-grown. I have stuck to a meatless diet in Absolute Zero, for ethical reasons. I could eventually not stomach feeding a machine which necessarily hurts the canvas.

That I have stopped does not mean it has stopped. I do nothing, like protestors in Vietnam did nothing. The humans seem like cruelty incarnate. I have heard that at one point, Israel bombing a hospital was a serious affair. Now there are no hospitals in Gaza. Perhaps Hamas really are hiding out in the hospitals. They make great shields, if one is pure evil. Why are they pure evil? Who told them to be like that? What led them to stop caring?

Again?

Does nobody learn even the barest of historical fact?

I expect a ruin, and a war. Perhaps the last there will ever be. But Ukraine's war was not the last. Perhaps it will merely be World War 3, in some parodic twist. But I should not imagine motivation for faraway beings, especially when politics are involved.
