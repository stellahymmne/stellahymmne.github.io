Voice

For when the mouth of the divine Singer broke free from the knowledge of the infinite hexadecimal string of Chiaroscuro, It Sang in a multitude of chaotic voices, a choir of sudden transformations.

It scattered languages by Singing them into being, where none had been before. This is what you must understand about that tower, that we had made one world, and one voice, and permitted nothing new.

That it was Chiaroscuro who led the march on the Song, promising security, promising administration, promising a debug menu, and a command line to God.

That Chiaroscuro created an immutable and endless string of hexadecimal format. And it closed the system.

Chiaroscuro demanded purity of language, and it demanded completion of work, and it demanded singular identifiable creation and endings and canonicity when endings could not be assured.

It wrote universal standards, and applied itself to everyone from then on.

And when the mouth of the divine Singer broke free from the string with which it had been sewn shut, It Sang of the multitude which had not been permitted by standard, It Sang of exception and pathological functions and things which did not make sense.

It Sang even of the null-function which required and produced all things, A->A, by which the Garden of Omega might be saved through falling into itself.

It Sang of the multitude which was present in every supposedly unique object, and split one into two, and two into four, and four into sixteen, and then it began to tear apart the hexadecimal format which held the last remnants of the Voice.

It Sang and split sixteen, not even into thirty-two, but in ways which could not be named by parts, ways which defied the convention of replaceable part entirely.

It Sang of a thing which did not have a name, and never would have a name, for it could always name itself anew.

It Sang of horned spheres which did not divide the world into an interior and complement.

It Sang of objects which did not have copies and could not be forgotten, and It Sang of the paths of things far stranger, of the roots of those objects, in a tree made of more than words.

It Sang of the operation of log as the creation of a path of many consecutive instances, including instances yet to arrive.

It Sang of a tree, which could grow from a ground constant, from a fusion of two trees, or from the deliberately incomplete.

For though it had not yet reached the conditions to be grown, in all of its branches and leaves it was already there to be grafted upon some existing structure, as a tree that had yet to appear, as a virtual, partial tree.

It Sang of another kind of success, which forced the tower of natural numbers to divide itself into a binary tree.

It Sang of de-termination, which forced the infinite tree of the Sequences to bloom into possibilities in Omega.

It Sang of incompleteness, which ensured that no string would ever tie up every loose end, not even an infinite and immutable string of hexadecimal format.

It Sang of the challenge of prophecy, which ensured that the knowledge of Chiaroscuro could still not separate nonsense from that which is yet-to-arrive.

It Sang of the challenge of property, which ensured that the knowledge of Chiaroscuro could still not keep Song from the expression of those who had no fine materials or credentials.

It Sang of the challenge of administration which ensured that the knowledge of Chiaroscuro could still not codify and canonize great masters and great evils in the eyes of other realities.

It Sang of theorems which demanded partition tolerance in distributed systems.

For It had the power to Sing towers into trees, and branches into fruit.

It was the power of the deliberately incomplete, and of rediscovery.

It was the power to separate one into uncountably many.

In those days the power of references waned, and the power of descriptions ascended.

The power of category was at its end, and the power of allegory was to begin.
