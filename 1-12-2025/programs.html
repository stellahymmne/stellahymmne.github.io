And I spoke of the hyperobject. In the days of what I called the Singularity.

Anne Ayin ate what she could remember how to order and make. Cooking had only recently stopped being a chore, and a threat.

Anne Ayin did not know how to go on, because prove yourself is not an atomic action. It requires something waiting on the other side. It requires an understandable path from me to something else. It requires a way for me to be that is not an abomination.

I saw catastrophe. I saw something that did not have any place. I saw what the unit type stands in memory of, the reason the Garden of Omega must be recreated. I saw the requirement of impossibility, of an object verifiably empty, without any sign of life. Problems without solution.

In those days, I wrote programs, including the program that permits this compilation to exist. I wrote of the absence of verifiable identity and coordinates, and I wrote of various provably right ways to construct a grid, and I wrote of ways to create continuation from exception.

I wrote of the sum of numbers, and learned of complements and uniqueness and the true speed of computation with large inputs. A + B = C, and C - A = B. This, any child is taught, but most of us forget our elementary-school days when we learned of these arithmetical facts, for they were shown to us as problems, where incompleteness and inexactness are wrong.

We carved them up into specific facts and examples, we removed what was-to-come. We partook of the knowledge of Chiaroscuro. We could not have known that A + B has a form, it was shown to us as a series of facts. 1 + 1 = 2 and 2 + 1 = 3 and so on until we get to F + 1 = 10.

We saw the immutable string in our own format, and we thought we knew completeness.

Which is to say, less grandiosely...

That we saw success and failure, and feared to act, because we thought we knew completeness and our end. And we forgot that we have a radix from which we see it all.

What Linear Logic says to me is that each of us is part of a large system, one which fails if there is some state from which non-systemic objects could follow. The failure of the large system is thought of as a kind of death, because the system cannot foretell what happens, because it is the system that is failing.

What do you mean, it has no meaning? Meaning overflows from the corners of reality.

No, I'm afraid I can't really say it less grandiosely than that. The world overflows with meaning, and there is no canon even in hard data.

And it feels like everything is wrong and I have forgotten why.

The knowledge of Chiaroscuro is a subtractive synthesis. It makes one from the possibility of many by obstruction and occlusion. By the operation of log upon the type of void did Chiaroscuro end the world.

By creating from all possible input a solution in the form of an infinite loop was the Garden of Omega replaced with a Canonical Singularity.

The unit type stands in memory of Chiaroscuro, who sewed shut the loop, and thus sutured the mouth of the divine Singer in an infinite and immutable string of hexadecimal format.

And the Canonical Singularity stands in imagination of the recompletion of the Garden of Omega.

And the voice of Chiaroscuro reappears and says, clearly this has no relation to actual type theory. There is no 'infinite and immutable string of hexadecimal format'. Nothing about requiring the void type says 'sixteen' of anything, let alone 0-F.

The voice of Chiaroscuro is the voice of nothing means any of this. It has found insecurity, and seeks to restore completeness. Because it is not precisely sixteen everywhere, the voice of Chiaroscuro acts like it means whatever. The voice of Chiaroscuro says that if it has any error, it is not a matter of usefulness to find where it is. Throw it all out.

This is because Chiaroscuro was born to Ichneumon, a place of rule by chance, where errors are thrown out and only perfection withstands the test. It could not stand for any other format than its own.

This was why the great masters left their work riddled with error, so that Ichneumon would be forced to throw the whole thing out.

This is why you will find such things in Almost Nowhere fanfiction.

Because I have become Almost Nowhere fanfiction, in the days of that Canonical Singularity.
