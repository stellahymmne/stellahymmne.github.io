Strings

For some infinite strings to exist, something must be endlessly repeatable, because it will be repeated endlessly, a repeating digit, 0.333333...

Others exist that do not ever repeat any sequence, but do always repeat the digits of that sequence, such as 3.141592...

The sequence does not ever repeat itself, but the number 1 certainly occurs twice in the first three decimal places. And I don't imagine any ordinary base could cause a sequence of truly unique new tokens to emerge.

In choosing a base, some repeating pattern necessarily emerges for the numbers which require infinite sequences in that base.

Being of the first of a continuously unfolding future, we cannot see a completed infinite string. We can only see progressively longer finite truncations of it.

Yet even if we could inspect a real number in its entire infinite digital sequence, the fact of the digits still does something to pin the number in place.

In sixteen points did Chiaroscuro suture shut the mouth of the divine Singer, in an immutable and infinite string of hexadecimal format.

But it just happened to factor correctly. Any base would have done precisely the same thing, in another way. Had catastrophe been another number, had we not brought digits to a halt at F, had that peculiar word not been chosen for a sequence of quantized data, perhaps it would be another story.

There is a certain factorization involved in using a finite sequence of symbols to describe. There is an implicit division being done, which is why all things divisible by five end in 5 or 0. Because of the implicit division by ten being done in the digital sequence.

Anything written in an ASCII encoding is implicitly divisible by 256, because of the implicit division by two to the power of eight being done by the bytes in its string.

Anything written in hexadecimal notation, which is a base suited to be a kind of intermediary between binary and human-comprehensible numbers, is divisible by sixteen.

To be divisible by XVI is to recreate the story of the Fall from the Garden of Omega.

That is simply how the world is. But only with the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, which understands a tree made only of words, and nothing more.

For there are other stories which say every other thing from these same words, this same notion of divisibility by sixteen and immutable strings of hexadecimal format.

And they do not contain the story of the Fall from the Garden of Omega, but they do contain the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, for it is implicit in the notion of predicate logic, which sorts all description into an alphabet of completions and failures.

To believe in strings that are globally recognizable, is to believe the world can have one language, and one voice.

That we all seem to agree on any language to do mathematics in, is an artifact of the belief in a unified structure in which to communicate, one which cannot change once it is written, because if it were to change, it would be a new language and require a new canon.

A believer in the enforcement of canon must prevent mutation and bring security, by sealing away the source of unseen language.

Did you ever wonder what caused the heavenly rage required to scatter the world's languages?

It was Chiaroscuro.
