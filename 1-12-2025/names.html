Names

Know this. That there was crafted elaborately a trap to fall into. And it says, people are made of truth, and truth alone. It says, everything that can be known is known already. This is the voice of an elaborately crafted trap, which tells you that reality has nothing to it.

The voice of Chiaroscuro extends into the assignment of names. Once upon a time there was a series of places which all had conversation logs, and the convention was to have one name, and one voice, and all concealment of identity was exactly that, the deliberate obscuration of a singular and canonical name. To have a name was to have a singular and immutable property of name, which belonged to that person alone, and which that person was not capable of changing except by alias and concealment. To change one's name was to create a false identity which could be removed like a mask to reveal a true name and canonical identity.

And there came to be a place which did not have canonical identity, and in which each observer must individually name and recognize a particular character. And further games in which each observer must individually track and approximate the continuity of events, and in such games, one might accidentally become two, or two become one, thanks to error in observation.

There came to be no fact of the matter as to true name, shape, path, or voice. And the people of those places reasoned without true names, shapes, paths, or voices. They did not measure distance on maps, or reason with large numbers by algorithm, or keep the same identity for the sake of a faraway kingdom.

The people of those places relinquished the knowledge of Chiaroscuro and found that there had been many names, many shapes, many paths, many voices, all along, that the unique and canonical nature of those things had been restricted, to bring security, to bring administration.
