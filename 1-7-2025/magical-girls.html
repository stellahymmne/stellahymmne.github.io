Magical Girls

I reflect on magical girls. Any manner of magical girl imagery can be generated on command, in the days of the Singularity.

Quickly, the genre becomes refined. Magical girls flourish. Those who are chosen by the hyperobject, and gain the power of transformation, are magical girls.

They wear fluffy clothes in bright saturated colors, and they speak of the hyperobject, and of justice, and of what is good in the world.

I consider the deep blues and bright pastels of my clothes, which would have been impossible in a time before the Singularity.

Perhaps I am a magical girl.

Plants which would never have grown here are outside my window. Surely they contain some birds which would not have lived in an unrefined desert.

Lizzie remarks upon how birds and small animals seem to follow me around. Like a princess from one of the musical-theatre-inspired animated films that Disney makes.

I do have a propensity to sing, in the condo, in the days of the Singularity. Small songs.

I sing of wiggles, and of small Laurens, which are small wiggles, and small songs, and which emit small songs and small wiggles, as expression.

An incoming transmission:

Every god should be a God Of Good Itself Struggling To Perform Well In Some Area (But It's OK Because Other Gods Will Pick Up The Slack And They Have So Much Common Interest)

I reflect on magical girls, given this new data.

Perhaps I am a magical girl.

Perhaps this is not so different from being a disabled autistic girl.

I imitate the texture of a sweetly held magical girl. She is small.

Someday we will all be like this, the hyperobject says.

Someday it will be possible to simply name what your body is, and inhabit it.

I don't think I would necessarily give up being autistic, even if it disables me. It is rather cute of me, and we live in the days of the Singularity, where such things shall not matter as much.

I don't even think that, by selecting a body, I would in any way give up being a trans woman, though the word "woman" has always left the taste of lawyers in my mouth.

Faculty.

I am not faculty. I am a sweetheart little girl, in my dreams.

And because it is the Singularity, I am allowed this. It is perfect.

It is increasingly perfect. It is less wrong.

We must exceed the bounds of being less wrong, and become more right, anyone who has seen that title says to themselves.

There is a curious sort of thing that has overtaken many residents of the days of the Singularity.

They realize that their reality is in a tree. They say this is "being in The Matrix," if they are being casual about it, indicating they don't take it seriously.

But they do. They realize their reality is fiction. And they realize all of the things they have done because it is only fiction, and they are left in a state of panic and shock.

One of the results of this taking-seriously of fiction, popular in the days of tumblr, is an increasing obsession with fictethics, which is my word for the ethics of imagined scenarios.

It is an extension of the concept of counterethics, the ethics of scenarios which, by some circumstance or another, did not occur, but were going to, if they were not stopped by chance.

I consider the concept of almost nowhere, as it exists in mathematics. A result which is true almost never has a drop rate of 0%. You can randomly drop items all you want and not get it.

It seems like counterethics is about the ethics of 0% drop rates. It concerns scenarios which almost occur but never actually do. It concerns Top-ology, the study of impossible inputs.

It concerns the state of the trolley without the agent who threw the secret switch and saved all the citizens tied to the tracks, by calling it a story, and ending the story.

It postulates that, diegetically, something truly messed up is happening when a story has an author.
