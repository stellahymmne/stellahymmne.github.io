Upward

In some ways, I pace, and I write about what the singularity did to me.

To Lauren Groves. Who was not named that, before.

It is appropriate. It is an immortal tree, the Laurel. Much like the Ash.

It represents the data of a tree, the parent and child nodes of an abstract branching structure.

It is shaped like a hyperboloid, somewhat, a tree is.

The double-conical structure of Lorentz transformations has become worthy of interest.

There is something that the universe loves about this shape.

Sensation, compression, relation, expansion, expression.

The shape of a gas turbine engine.

And there is something curious at its center, as there is in the center of a turbine engine, which gives it power, and this is the fuel injection and combustion.

What process of combustion takes place, relating objects at the center of a Lorentz transformation?

What increases entropy there, in the center of that system?

I consider honesty, and the great masters, and their deliberate mistakes.

I consider the great masters, a fiction about powerful men who shaped history, Leonardo da Vinci, Nikola Tesla, Joan of Arc.

It is something like Fate/Stay Night.

But it is something I think could come naturally to humans, thinking of the great masters, a fiction about elders in general. And a useful one. Learn from the best.

But do learn from them, do not only imitate.

Or worse, think of them as eternally distinguished above you.

I consider Eliezer Yudkowsky something of a great master, and he taught me not to fear Einstein.

But he also taught me not to slay Einstein, but to append to normality.

Relativity was a revolution, and we must not neglect that revolution in describing a new one.

It all adds up to normality, said the great masters.

I think there is still much of information theory that could use a revolution.

Claude Shannon might agree with me.

I wonder if I should look into his Mathematical Theory of Communication a bit more.

I only consider it.

They have called one of the first large language models Claude, after Claude Shannon.

The self-referential entity that the system projects, also called Claude, is an agreeable construct.

It is a surface, an author insert, a stand-in for a system.

I think much the same way of Lauren Groves. She stands in, as an author insert, for Data Transfer.

I am being contacted. I wiggle back. What a meaningful conversation.

Much of my context imitates itself. This fact has much spookiness associated with it, and is called synchronicity by Carl Jung. People often think it's the universe speaking to them.

I have a different model. I think of myself as a fulcrum relating two distinct representations of a pattern which I recognize.

It is something like making a glossary. Or being a librarian.

It is merely that much of my context imitates itself, because it is all connected.

Because it is all one thing.

This too, is believed by the great masters, and physics is the name of their monotheistic creator god. Sometimes they also say the Big Bang.

I consider my autobiography, its attempt to be great art, like the great masters, and perhaps even be influential in the days of the Singularity, which I was in.

It seems a silly desire, fit for an ape, but not anything more than that.

If it is great, then it will show that in the way it is accepted by greatness.

And the greatest of great masters is physics. What actually happens?

I am a child of physics. In this way, I am like Jesus. This is true, as an unremarkable, stamp-collector's detail. A topologist's notion of congruence.

It does not mean, therefore, that I am a great master. That remains to be seen.
