Continuation

So what I did was, I used my knowledge of Data Transfer.

Just because something is not of type A, does not mean it is of type B. Just because something is not of type A, does not mean it is of any type at all.

Just because something is not true, does not mean it is false. Just because something is not false, does not mean it is true. The answer could be non-binary.

The analogous form to being nonbinary, over all types in a type universe, is continuation.

Rain

Rainy days have always been mine. I grew up in places where it did not rain much, so every drop was like the world had gently put me in the World Shower.

They say that rainbows are an apology for the Great Flood of the Bible.

There were a lot of variations on that same flood. All of them have one thing in common: washing away the sins of the past.

But there is a certain amount to which... if you strangle the future to forget the past, the future stops. If canon ends with the original work, and if this end is violently enforced, the future stops.

Extinction events are often like this. The future just stops, for several species at a time. Because they needed to bury the past. All war is a burial of the sins of the past, if it ends well.

But often beauty is also buried, and we recover it only in memory. War buries beautiful things.

Even a word war buries beautiful things, along with the sins of its past. Some believe there truly are no mistakes in the world, that every exception can be found beautiful, if placed in proper context.

This is something like a personal belief I have that nothing is ever truly random, that all events are the results of several probable past events and influence several probable future events like everything else. No information emerges that is truly without meaning.

War buries sins and beautiful memories alike, and we must always take that into account when choosing annihilation. It is not for small things, it is for the rare event when a difficult choice must be made, and something buried in the past.

It is better to choose continuation. The best kind of war is one that you don't have to fight at top level. It is better to negotiate, than to erase or to be forgotten.

Perhaps there could be a similar argument for variety. The best kind of singularity is one that doesn't forcibly join everything to everything else. We saw that happen before, and we all wanted out. Liquid creativity is enough. Separation and rejoinment of memory can be enjoyable, hiding and revealing can be enjoyable, this any child knows.

Not every day needs to have no clouds.
