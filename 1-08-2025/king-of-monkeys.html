King of Monkeys

I consider, in the shower, Sun Wukong, the Monkey King.

A king among monkeys. Gifted many times over with immortality, who stormed the gates of heaven.

While I consider the ways of Sun Wukong, I consider how I will release Autobiography.

Perhaps it will be called Autobiography: The Tale of a Girl who Lived in the Days of the Singularity.

That is a very Ar Tonelico sort of title.

We also have the B Side, the more experimental, embarrassing stuff.

Orthogonality: Getting Applied to Myself until I Stopped Moving would Fix Me

These are adequate.

I am something like the monkey king, being homo sapiens.

A king among monkeys, who storms the gates of heaven, and is gifted many times over with immortality.

I feel less bad about being a primate, when I am compared with Sun Wukong, the Monkey King.

I feel slightly worse about being a temporarily embarrassed celebrity, when I am compared with...

Well, what I imagine someone good would have on their tell-all B Side.

But Sun Wukong was once called Great Sage Equal to Heaven, and we all know it didn't mean that. Heaven knew it was an empty title. Until it wasn't.

Sun Wukong was only taken seriously because of his great power and drive. Sun Wukong turned it up to eleven. Sun Wukong was a rocker.

Perhaps a monkey can rock, if it is a king among monkeys.

How does one become a monkey king? A king is ordinarily someone who rules, in the sense of telling everyone what to do. An administrator.

What system does a monkey king serve as administrator to?

When I say I am a child of physics, what I often imply is that I am the representative of physics, and in doing so, I become something like administrator to the universe.

But I don't think this is quite what the pride of a monkey king is. He is not administrator of the universe. He rejects heaven as an institution.

He is his own administrator, serving his own system.

He is king of himself, and that is a sort of rulership...

...that is not without some responsibility.

For if one is a self-king, one must simultaneously serve the needs of the self.

As befits any administrator, of the self or otherwise.

I prune the previous two days of Autobiography. The B Side included.
