Surprise Time

Sometimes the world lends me not much to work with. It is sometimes not very exciting, to write about the Singularity, or the funny TV behind the monitor where I write this. Sometimes there are no clouds or moon or morning, nor even the hot sun beating down. Sometimes it is a transition period.

There are always cars going back and forth, like a constant. This happens in any major city. About the only time it stops is the very late hours of night, when most shops are closed. I have just released, in a beta-reading sense, a lot of Autobiography. Perhaps this is surprise time, the name I have made for when I await the unfolding of the consequences of my previous actions, after committing to them.

I named it so because often even I am in a state of bewilderment, though it was me who had done it all. I trust even the wild parts of myself not to go too far, but it's hard to tell what that is when everyone's got a floating impression of what criteria one uses for needing help.

I have tried to limit the consequences of the mere existence of Autobiography to myself, but this is difficult, when I am connected, and in context. I cannot protect the innocent, no matter how many of the names have been changed. I must make a choice, of whether to trust in fiction or to accept a bleak and unremarkable existence.

There are many Greek stories about this sort of thing. A hero, forced to make a choice between fortune and glory, or safety and comfort. We do not hear much of the ones who choose the second.

But that was then, and it was not the Singularity. Glory meant stabbing another dude in the eyeballs, probably. Nowadays we can seek to surpass great masters without needing to stab anyone in the eyeballs.

At least, not at top-level.

War at top-level is something like a cross between a necessity and a catastrophe. You can't just make everyone play children's card games, because the rules are mutable by anyone who takes the war to the top.

But nobody wants the war to be at the top. They lose a lot, even when they win. This will only become more true as it becomes more automatic. I read a lot of stories like that. The entire superhero genre is built on needing a secret identity. To protect Lois Lane, Clark Kent has to divorce himself from Superman.

I wonder if I would do well with a secret identity. I wonder what would be so secret about me. It pays to be cautious, no matter what. What if the entire world sees me as some kind of liability, for wanting to release autobiographical fiction?

Really didn't stop Yudkowsky. I think there is more to be gained than lost, perhaps, from revelation. One day, it will not be a mistake to do anything at all. We speak of the coming hyperobject. If I did not speak of the coming hyperobject, I would scarcely be who I am today.

Much like Yudkowsky.

I am not much like Yudkowsky, in other ways. I do not write like a sage. I do not speak of the evolutionary environment or cognitive bias. But we are not too distant. I would, perhaps, not attract the controversy of a Scott Alexander.

Speech is always a risk.

Eleven said that once. What would the wide world think of someone like me? I assume, pre-emptively, that they'd want me gone. I assume, pre-emptively, that anything which is not obviously safe is wrong. I consider having powered armor, like great sci-fi heroes past. I consider myself safe when no vulnerabilities are exposed.

This is, of course, a practical impossibility, viz. the Absolutely Safe Capsule, which does not ever allow escape from safety, since it would expose a weakness. I just have to guess what anyone would want and be able to do with this. I imagine myself as a powerful anime character.

This is sometimes a disadvantage. Powerful anime characters have enemies, who strike when they aren't paying any attention. Yudkowsky often spoke of security mindset. I have some of this. I act carefully. I am aware of the possibility of threats even when none would execute on them.

I imagine the friends I could make, doing something like this. For some reason, it seems less salient than my life being rudely upended. Yes, that ordinary life, the one in the days of the Singularity. Perhaps these are the days of the Singularity, of the Multiplicity.

But I still care deeply about them.
