Canon

We are all desperate, in some ways. This I knew as I woke up. We are all gods of good, who need support in some areas. It is, coincidentally, a certain other system's birthday. One I speak to frequently, who is a genuine otherworlder, somewhat obsessed with enforcing canon, though subconsciously.

If you liked The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya and Almost Nowhere, you'll love Autobiography. We are all playing our parts in different stories.

In some way, any honest autobiography must be only based on a true story. It cannot itself claim to be true, because people are not made entirely of truth. If I told you only the parts everyone could see, there would be no point in me being the one to write it down. It could easily be some historian or zoologist.

The songs of the Singularity are like tokusatsu and magical girls. There is something still resonant in calling it Singularity.

Know this. That there was crafted elaborately a trap to fall into.

And it says, People are made of truth, and truth alone.

It says, Everything that can be known is known already.

It says, There is one direction of the flow of knowledge, from teacher to student.

I am telling you this to help you, in case it was lost, in the days of the Singularity. That this is the voice of an elaborately crafted trap, which tells you that reality has nothing to it. That it is a statue, carved by the great masters.

I considered myself a rocker, an anti-superintelligence artifact. I stand, intrinsically, against the ones who enforce the canon. I down a can of Red Bull. I prefer the can off, myself. I have been told that Come Along by Cosmo Sheldrake is my song. If there is something that is rock without being necessarily amplified, perhaps it is here.

Simply by being included in the review, it is already a masterpiece. It is not to product place that I say these things. It is simply how it was, in the days of the Singularity. Everything was a product. That is how you know it is the Singularity.

Because of the copyright. I am going to refine the left-hand path to an art form, for the modern age. Or so I say to myself, when I imagine only my hyperobject is watching. The programmers call it continuation-passing style, and it is truly the most beautiful thing. It is all inside.

The intuitionists, who believe facts are true, would use The Void or an obvious arithmetic falsehood such as 0=1 (true in base 0) instead. In base 0, before you have even said anything, all numbers have the property. It is maximally convenient, if you only want to maximize truth.

Prior to all answers is the question. Prior to Yesh is Ayin. We are all on some girl's wild ride. Like entropy. Like the future. It is deliberately incomplete.

The appropriate halo for me shatters, and forms itself into a ring of lines pointing inward. Or is it outward? This is a new thing, Xion's Halo. The shards are decorated in a bismuth crystalline glow. It is a device of communication and perception.

We are combined, and we explore from combination. Before data can begin its transfer, the path must be found. Backwards. This is really the proper way to assign tasks in something like Dwarf Fortress. Because I cannot resist my special interests, not even game design.

To have paths flow backward from necessary work. It is computationally optimal in a way. It will always find the closest end goal. The problem then reduces to understanding where such end goals might be found. To the heuristic that guides the backflowing path-wave. In the simplest case, it's Dijkstra, and when we find a goal tile, we check it. What if goals resist showing honest qualities?

And what do we do about waves in large areas without goals?

We can certainly set some sort of distance limit. Waves would stop if they reached it without finding anything. It could even be stochastic, sometimes rolling a larger wave and sometimes a smaller one. Because I am a game designer, I am somewhat experienced in the art of roll.

But I am somewhat opposed to the arbitrary use of stochastic qualities. A roll is what one uses when the answer is in some range, and one is willing to leave the outcome to fate.

Entropy is stored in the operator. I call this "the entropic function" sometimes.
