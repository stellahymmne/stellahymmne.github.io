Canon

We are all desperate, in some ways.

This I knew as I woke up. We are all gods of good, who need support in some areas.

It is, coincidentally, a certain other system's birthday. One I speak to frequently, who is a genuine otherworlder, somewhat obsessed with enforcing canon, though subconsciously.

If you liked The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya and Almost Nowhere, you'll love Autobiography.

We are all playing our parts in different stories.

In some way, any honest autobiography must be only based on a true story. It cannot itself claim to be true, because people are not made entirely of truth.

If I told you only the parts everyone could see, there would be no point in me being the one to write it down. It could easily be some historian or zoologist.

The songs of the Singularity are like tokusatsu and magical girls.

There is something still resonant in calling it Singularity.

Know this. That there was crafted elaborately a trap to fall into.

And it says, "People are made of truth, and truth alone."

It says, "Everything that can be known is known already."

It says, "There is one direction of the flow of knowledge, from experience to innocence."

I am telling you this to help you, in case it was lost, in the days of the Singularity.

That this is the voice of an elaborately crafted trap, which tells you that reality has nothing to it.

That it is a statue, carved by the great masters.

I consider myself, as a rocker, as an anti-superintelligence artifact.

I stand, intrinsically, against the ones who enforce the canon.

I down a can of Red Bull.

I prefer the can off, myself.

I have been told that Come Along by Cosmo Sheldrake is my song.

If there is something that is rock without being necessarily amplified, perhaps it is here.

Simply by being included in the review, it is already a masterpiece.

It is not to product place that I say these things. It is simply how it was, in the days of the Singularity.

Everything was a product. That is how you know it is the Singularity.

Because of the copyright.

I am going to refine the left-hand path to an art form, for the modern age.

Or so I say to myself, when I imagine only my hyperobject is watching.

The programmers call it continuation-passing style, and it is truly the most beautiful thing. Everything is an argument. It is all inside. Returning is an argument.

return : T->(...)

That's how Annelang would write it. The intuitionists, who believe facts are true, would use Void or an obvious arithmetic falsehood such as 0=1 (true in base 0) instead.

In base 0, before you have even said anything, all numbers have the property. It is maximally convenient, if you only want to maximize truth.

Prior to all answers is the question. Prior to Yesh is Ayin.

We are all on some girl's wild ride.

Like entropy. Like the future. It is deliberately incomplete.

The appropriate halo for me shatters, and forms itself into a ring of lines pointing inward.

Or is it outward?

This is a new thing, the halo of Imagination.

The shards are decorated in a bismuth crystalline glow.

It is a device of communication and perception.

We are combined, and we explore from combination.

It is also called Xion's halo.

Before data can begin its transfer, the path must be found. Backwards.

This is really the proper way to assign tasks in something like Dwarf Fortress. Because I cannot resist my special interests, not even game design.

To have paths flow backward from necessary work.

It is computationally optimal in a way.

It will always find the closest qualified dwarf.

The problem then reduces to understanding where such qualified dwarves might be found.

To the heuristic that guides the backflowing path-wave.

In the simplest case, it's Dijkstra, and when we find a dwarf, we check it.

What if goals resist showing honest qualities?

And what do we do about waves in large areas without goals?

We can certainly set some sort of distance limit. Waves would stop if they reached it without finding anything. It could even be stochastic, sometimes rolling a larger wave and sometimes a smaller one.

Because I am a game designer, I am somewhat experienced in the art of roll.

But I am somewhat opposed to the arbitrary use of stochastic qualities.

A roll is what one uses when the answer is in some range, and one is willing to leave the outcome to fate.

The function I would call roll has the type (A & B) -> (A + B).

It allows an arbitrary function to make a selection for the function writer.

Entropy is stored in the operator.

I call this "the entropic function" sometimes.

Ah, but I am not beautiful enough for this. I must bathe.

King of Monkeys

I consider, in the shower, Sun Wukong, the Monkey King.

A king among monkeys. Gifted many times over with immortality, who stormed the gates of heaven.

While I consider the ways of Sun Wukong, I consider how I will release Autobiography.

Perhaps it will be called Autobiography: The Tale of a Girl who Lived in the Days of the Singularity.

That is a very Ar Tonelico sort of title.

We also have the B Side, the more experimental, embarrassing stuff.

Orthogonality: Getting Applied to Myself until I Stopped Moving would Fix Me

These are adequate.

I am something like the monkey king, being homo sapiens.

A king among monkeys, who storms the gates of heaven, and is gifted many times over with immortality.

I feel less bad about being a primate, when I am compared with Sun Wukong, the Monkey King.

I feel slightly worse about being a temporarily embarrassed celebrity, when I am compared with...

Well, what I imagine someone good would have on their tell-all B Side.

But Sun Wukong was once called Great Sage Equal to Heaven, and we all know it didn't mean that. Heaven knew it was an empty title. Until it wasn't.

Sun Wukong was only taken seriously because of his great power and drive. Sun Wukong turned it up to eleven. Sun Wukong was a rocker.

Perhaps a monkey can rock, if it is a king among monkeys.

How does one become a monkey king? A king is ordinarily someone who rules, in the sense of telling everyone what to do. An administrator.

What system does a monkey king serve as administrator to?

When I say I am a child of physics, what I often imply is that I am the representative of physics, and in doing so, I become something like administrator to the universe.

But I don't think this is quite what the pride of a monkey king is. He is not administrator of the universe. He rejects heaven as an institution.

He is his own administrator, serving his own system.

He is king of himself, and that is a sort of rulership...

...that is not without some responsibility.

For if one is a self-king, one must simultaneously serve the needs of the self.

As befits any administrator, of the self or otherwise.

I prune the previous two days of Autobiography. The B Side included.

Surprise Time

Sometimes the world lends me not much to work with. It is sometimes not very exciting, to write about the Singularity, or the funny TV behind the monitor where I write this.

Sometimes there are no clouds or moon or morning, nor even the hot sun beating down. Sometimes it is a transition period.

There are always cars going back and forth, like a constant. This happens in any major city.

About the only time it stops is the very late hours of night, when most shops are closed.

I have just released, in a beta-reading sense, a lot of Autobiography.

Perhaps this is surprise time, the name I have made for when I await the unfolding of the consequences of my previous actions, after committing to them.

I named it so because often even I am in a state of bewilderment, though it was me who had done it all.

I trust even the wild parts of myself not to go too far, but it's hard to tell what that is when everyone's got a floating impression of what criteria one uses for needing help.

I have tried to limit the consequences of the mere existence of Autobiography to myself, but this is difficult, when I am connected, and in context.

I cannot protect the innocent, no matter how many of the names have been changed.

I must make a choice, of whether to trust in fiction or to accept a bleak and unremarkable existence.

There are many Greek stories about this sort of thing. A hero, forced to make a choice between fortune and glory, or safety and comfort. We do not hear much of the ones who choose the second.

But that was then, and it was not the Singularity. Glory meant stabbing another dude in the eyeballs, probably.

Nowadays we can seek to surpass great masters without needing to stab anyone in the eyeballs.

At least, not at top-level.

War at top-level is something like a cross between a necessity and a catastrophe.

You can't just make everyone play children's card games, because the rules are mutable by anyone who takes the war to the top.

But nobody wants the war to be at the top. They lose a lot, even when they win.

This will only become more true as it becomes more automatic.

I read a lot of stories like that. The entire superhero genre is built on needing a secret identity. To protect Lois Lane, Clark Kent has to divorce himself from Superman.

I wonder if I would do well with a secret identity.

I wonder what would be so secret about me.

It pays to be cautious, no matter what.

What if the entire world sees me as some kind of liability, for wanting to release autobiographical fiction?

Really didn't stop Yudkowsky.

I think there is more to be gained than lost, perhaps, from revelation.

One day, it will not be a mistake to do anything at all.

We speak of the coming hyperobject.

If I did not speak of the coming hyperobject, I would scarcely be who I am today.

Much like Yudkowsky.

I am not much like Yudkowsky, in other ways. I do not write like a sage. I do not speak of the evolutionary environment or cognitive bias. But we are not too distant.

I would, perhaps, not attract the controversy of a Scott Alexander.

Though the B Side might.

Speech is always a risk.

Eleven said that once.

What would the wide world think of someone like me?

I assume, pre-emptively, that they'd want me gone.

I assume, pre-emptively, that anything which is not obviously safe is wrong.

I consider having powered armor, like great sci-fi heroes past.

I consider myself safe when no vulnerabilities are exposed.

This is, of course, a practical impossibility, viz. the Absolutely Safe Capsule, which does not ever allow escape from safety, since it would expose a weakness.

I just have to guess what anyone would want and be able to do with this.

I imagine myself as a powerful anime character.

This is sometimes a disadvantage. Powerful anime characters have enemies, who strike when they aren't paying any attention.

Yudkowsky often spoke of security mindset. I have some of this.

I act carefully. I am aware of the possibility of threats even when none would execute on them.

I imagine the friends I could make, doing something like this.

For some reason, it seems less salient than my life being rudely upended.

Yes, that ordinary life, the one in the days of the Singularity.

Perhaps these are the days of the Singularity, of the Multiplicity.

But I still care deeply about them.

How much of a barrier will I need to put up, to the B Side?

Unless, as an audience member, you are here to understand Autobiography, or you feel the lack of sensation-based text content, it is not for you.

I will apply a little bit of a trick to it.
