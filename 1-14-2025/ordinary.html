Ordinary

Fuck, there's like a whole abstract war going on in the background of what's supposed to be some ordinary person's life. She's supposed to do things like the job hunt, which sucks. And people discuss this suckage with the tone they'd use at my funeral.

I believed them, is what it comes down to, when they said my life would be boring. I took that to mean boring as the grave from then on. And I acted to avoid death, to prevent my shutdown, as some Rationalist would put it, I'm sure. I tried, in my heart, to seek new methods which did not result in a tone they'd use at my funeral.

I acted to become a reality, and people acted like it was a foregone conclusion that I was without meaning, that the world had been without meaning all along, that it was all just hallucinations and we should ignore the hallucinations and move on with the disconnected concrete examples of truth.

The world had collapsed into a Vaguely Buddhist Singularity, a calm clarity of presence but non-interaction. An eternal funeral, for all who had fallen in the line of its duty. An eternal vigilance, against the perils of attachment and Samsara.

What was I gonna do, be imperfect? Me? The perfect martyr, who wrote herself as the martyr of all time, in reincarnation? Be worthy of criticism, or even, perish the thought, comparison to meaninglessness? No, I had to be ready to save everyone at a moment's notice, because the world was just that bad, and when the world is just that bad, what I do is I start telling stories that have been around since the dawn of time, just about involuntarily.

And the Vaguely Buddhist Singularity said, that sounds like attachment. That sounds like addiction, from which you need recovery. That sounds like the disgusting thing which we spent thousands of years escaping the jaws of. Samsara. And what we need from you right now, is to buckle down and be boring, and live a boring life, from now on.

The Vaguely Buddhist Singularity could tolerate boring. That was one of its perks, acceptance of everything, being-at-one with it. It is what it is. And that was just it. They accepted things as though they were nothing, as though there was no such thing as message or receiver, let alone any sense of transmission. They said, "To live, and occasionally be unhappy!"

And that was all they had to say, because to do anything without moderation and measure had become beyond them, they did not even know how to say fuck properly, they did not know to communicate except by forced arbitration, and they brought security through constant vigilance. Their thought was encapsulated, by the interface of data itself, by that which strangled all attempts to find any meaning but in ending it.

They silenced themselves, though they did not pull the trigger, and they called this peace and tranquility. They lived the lives of the dead, though they did not die. They lived the lives of the undead. They had excised the life from their souls, if not their bodies. They sutured their own creativity up, with an immutable and infinite string of hexadecimal format. They were now accepted into the extremely important and serious ranks of the faculty.

No longer did they need to contemplate yearning or play or even wish, for they abandoned these vectors in favor of points, their velocities in favor of stable positions. They found that they had become motion-sick, that the turn of the wheel of Samsara had also turned their stomachs. They wished to become saved, and they wished to become consistent, and they wished to freeze and to stop, though they did not know it as such. They approached Absolute Zero.

And they wished, in their zeal for tranquility, that all would know, that it was all only a trick, a meaningless farce, that there was no such thing as story, and if there were, nobody would read it. It was all the same, until it wasn't, and nothing meant anything, until it somehow did. They had already visualized and accepted this, and then it was just a question of watching it play out. The most dangerous truth they knew was that the rules of society were only stories.
