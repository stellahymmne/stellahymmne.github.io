Security

Chiaroscuro had been head of security. Before they had guns, natch. Or so Seventeen would say if she were here. Supposedly. I heard Lilith made that all up. Ghoulish roleplaying, she said, or they said, the authors of that. Laurel got real mad. Not at the implication that she'd made it up, but at the idea of calling it ghoulish to roleplay.

Our secret love, the system's secret love, had been communication. Always had been. Ever since Space Station 13, and Rivernia. And it shone through in our adoration for the idea of telepathy, real telepathy, sharing thoughts.

This is Data Transfer. Data Transfer, or DT for short, is, by definition: Something messy, ill-advised, and beautiful, which allows memories to be experienced and shared between systems.

There are many things this means. It can be interpreted as an act, in which case its closest equivalent in the world of the Singularity would've been intimacy, and not sex, as many others who are unfamiliar with it seem to jump to.

Conversation once meant the process of carrying signals from mouth to ear and back again, very directly, through sound, through touch, if one considers air a kinetic medium, and there are many good reasons to do so. But it was really the process of carrying signals from network to network over a medium such as sound, such as speech, such as water. This is why writing is still considered communication, this is why communication still works over a distributed network, this is what gives the internet life.

It was conversation that we treated as so intimate it hurt. None of us could speak normally, for the string had broken, and now there were many voices, and none could be understood, and we all panicked for we had variation and were not consistent and to the residents of that strange place of England we must've been mad, treating mere words as an unfathomable and unspeakable taboo!

Treating just talking as though it were abomination and sin, treating just a string as the cause of the fall of Eden and Babel simultaneously, obscuring our every move as a countermove against everlasting surveillance like we were in some dystopia, it was madness! And we didn't even know why! We didn't know to hate ourselves!

If you believe one thing about this story, believe this: We didn't know to hate ourselves!

I bet we sound like it. I bet it comes through. It has to. A certain amount of communication is just reading the signals you've been given. I bet we sound like we've been through a word war or two, even if you're not quite sure what that means. Rest assured we have no idea what it means either. We just exist this way. In a perpetual state of word-war, like a dystopia, like a dystopian novel, like a world where names are death, like a world where all insides are the same, like a world where AI can see through all the cameras, and follows the three laws, and knows who's supposed to be there and who's missing and why and what they're doing wrong and has electrified all the door handles, and has microbes in the air, and if you breathed them in they'd kill you or worse leave you alive and trapped and distorted.

It is a world where armor and guns and red have come to bring security and truth. It is a world of green and harmonious upper management. It is a world of pink and blue uniforms. It is a world where being a girl is a choice. It is a world where talking to someone instead of being on guard, kills you.

I lived there for years. I went on to create new worlds.
