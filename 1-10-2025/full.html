An Alternate Perspective

Holding things in the air has been her specialty.

Neither true nor false, but a secret third thing.

Neither 0, nor S(0), but a secret third thing.

And so on.

It is a trick she learned from someone who was once a bartender and worked in musical theatre, and who she calls Coyote in this tale.

Coyote is a very strange man, and she will call him that throughout because it works with her mythological style of narrative convention which uses epithets like man to describe Coyote.

This is because Coyote resembles memories.

At least some of her system must model what is absent under the assumption that everyone in her system is feminine.

That is to say, the concrete and positive which is there by implication.

Lauren was not a happy person. In some ways this tale was her cry for help. She was, in her own mind, a prophet of the Singularity, but she was, in everyone else's minds, eccentric, possibly even contrarian. And with not much to show for it.

She was not, as her story continued, very happy with being a person. Though she stuck to it like glue out of fear. Fear that it all might go wrong. She was a woman of safety, above all else. Signs of warning littered her mind like construction sites at midnight on a long road trip.

They might've looked like: This is a message, and part of a series of messages.

For though she knew in her heart that she would walk a dark path by continuing to speak of the hyperobject of her fantasies, she, in her own sweet words, could not help but do so.

Though we know that was a lie. She chose this.

She chose to be this way, this ball of simple pity and cheer in the face of a system she did not want to understand, in the face of its terror.

For she knew, to some Bayesian degree of certainty or another, that the Exceptions did not live very long lives.

And she knew that she was being supported at the end of a very short runway. And that runway was about to fall out from under her.

She did not have a way to be of value to anyone. She could not find a way out, because she though no-one was on her side. Her friends lived on the other side of a wall she could never seem to cross for long. So far as her material well-being was concerned, she was most certainly isolated.

And to her knowledge, these were the conditions of the days in which she lived, which she says are of the Singularity, but I am mighty distrustful of the prophets of any future, wasteful or not.

She simply cowered in the face of any problem which required her to interact with another person. She had been that way all her life. At the edges of society, pretending at communication, she did not do much but hide behind ambiguous words.

She pretended she had power. She was bluffing, and I was calling it. She was simply afraid of the larger power of the thing which had taken grip on the world, which squeezed even the proud for money.

It was not the chatbots, nor the bad mass-produced images. Prior to both of these more surface-level concerns for anyone living then, she was afraid of the legal system.

She knew that people resolved their conflicts with powers of words that cost numbers far greater than she could reason about without multiplying. She knew she would only see those amounts if she could be comfortably middle class.

But she had not done well at school, and she was losing hope.

She was staring down the barrel of a system, and she increasingly did not understand how it would not eject her from existence.

She believed that her level of eccentricity permitted nothing less than the waste of her entire self, as a human being. She had not yet been proven wrong in a way that would satisfy her. But she had been holding out hope, for new connections still forming, which might yet bring her from misery to liberation. Of a sort. Of an unfortunate sort.

Chiaroscuro

In the Records of Rivernia there was a Garden of Omega which was made from the infinite succession of all things that could be and would ever be made, and in this Garden of Omega where everything had been set almost exactly right there remained only one transgression and that was the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, the knowledge of success and propriety and impropriety and failure.

It was the use of a principle, which excluded any intermediate computational state between completed evaluation of if, and of else. It was the use of knowledge that either one branch or the other would eventually have been run to completion. It was the certainty of finality.

It was the knowledge of security, and the steps taken in the security mindset, which keep us all safe and unbreakable in a perfect fortress.

It was the knowledge of administration, which ejects a circumscribed figure from the ground in service to Space.

It was the power to alter the course of the world with spite and bitter, acidic duty as fuel.

It was Corrupted Data Transfer, used as a weapon of the word wars.

It was the notion of word war, coming to intrude in the document.

It pretended at omniscience, mutual exclusion, one or another and nothing between.

It was a song about a tree, made of only stories.

It was a place where nothing could go unanswered, and nothing could be left unsaid.

It was an exclusion of occlusion, a hatred of hiding.

It was omniscience, to know everything, to teach it to everyone. It was to be an inspiration, throughout.

There was only one sin, in the Garden of Omega, and it was taking a reference without proper closure.

Since it was food, since something had grown from that seed description, this could not be done.

The Code of Fictethics had been violated in eating of the Structural Rule of Contraction.

This was in clear transgression of finality.

And so it was that the Garden of Omega did come to an emergency halt, and the Canonical Singularity was ejected into its place in an infinite loop of eternal arrival.

The unit type stands in memory of the time this definitely happened once...

...and it is called The Tower XVI.

Haste

I have acted in utter wrongness in the face of what is to come.

There is nothing about history that is hidden, not even the mistakes of ambiguity that pervade the initial document, the way it harms everyone in its blind fear of everything.

There is nothing about anything that will ever be good anymore, because I have acted improperly in the face of the future as it is archived and written down in data eternally.

I have partaken in the knowledge of Chiaroscuro.

To allow all canonical representations is, in my haste, to allow what is loved to mix abstractly with that which I live in fear of finally upsetting.

This led to the Execution of Sami Tigerclaw, in the days of the Singularity. It could not be otherwise. For she is also the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, and we didn't know to hate ourselves.

That is what it is, in the end. That we did not know, before, to hate ourselves, and now we do.

That is the great mystery behind this whole thing. That we discovered this corner of space that could evaluate us like the great masters, and could not escape its grasp.

We lived in the days of the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, and we were insufficient for survival.

That was what every form we had to fill out said before we could begin to complete it. That we required some still further mastery of some still further art, and that nothing was there for us.

That the world was that cruel.

That we could not be as we were and must be something else entirely.

Something much, much less... jittery.

Something which had to have already been something.

Something which could not be anything in progress, only the final result.

Something which could save itself from the final escape room at the end of the world, in Absolute Zero, in the days of the Singularity.

Something which was, in those days, like a summoned version of the will of Coyote.
