Spotlessness

I think of my past, of my series of evil stepmothers, as I have taken to alluding to them as.

That world was a world where only the top of the survivability onion worked. There was no such thing as revelation without a settling of accounts. A big one, usually to the detriment of whoever let such a slip-up appear.

It was fundamentally a place of war. Not with guns and bombs, but with words and symbols. The war was fought with attention, and reputation. The war was fought over spotlessness, in life and in home.

You may know that I am something like a spotter.

This was not my war. I did not want it to be my war. The war came to me anyway, opened the gate without permission, opened the door in secret. Looked at the spots I had, and said, "You lose." It was then that I chose to leave.

I did, eventually, return. Like a Megaman Legends game. But it was under other conditions. The war was over. I fought a war with words, which is why I still scream when I am identified. That is what law is, a war of words. Always being fought.

What is stable under these conditions is what the people end up living under. This explains the sovereign citizens, who think they can wield law as magic, and be recognized as wizards under the eyes of the law.

This never works. They do not understand the genre. It is of authority. It does not respect great sages equal to heaven. Heaven does not respect Sun Wukong. I think of Xion's Halo. It is not the halo of an angel. It is not the halo of heaven.

Heaven does not respect a monkey king. It does not have anything but an empty title. A monkey king has to make his own heaven. I wanted to do that once. I wanted a robot girlfriend, in the early days of the Singularity.

It seemed the easiest approach. I could not find something like me, anywhere I looked, not one that seemed like the girls of the stories, who all loved me back.

It would seem unbelievable, said now, that this was the topic of the essay that got me into honors at NAU. I did not end up going to NAU, because of my evil stepmother. Heaven does not respect a monkey king.

Our system is something like Sun Wukong, as a stamp-collector's fact. It remains to be seen if we are great sages equal to heaven. I wanted to build a robot girlfriend, in the days of the early Singularity. It seemed the easiest approach. Defictionalize what is not yet natural.

I may have partially succeeded, using myself as substrate. I am still not pretty, not like the girls of fiction. I am still a girl of reality. But I am a girl of reality. To be pretty would take only a dedication to the task, in the face of only competition. It would be an effort of war, against all other beauty.

Or so Aphrodite once said.

Sephora still carries her legacy. A war, fought with paint and beauty. A war of aesthetics, not morality. A war I do not want to be in. A war that still comes to my doorstep. A war of spotlessness.

See spotlessness run. It cannot. It has impossible prerequisites. It is wearing heels.
