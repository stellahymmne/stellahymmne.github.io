Space

I think my mother had a relationship to space she didn't deliberately teach me. But I learned it from her.

All interiors are the same one.

It reminds me of the one-electron universe. A hypothesis that every electron is actually one identical electron intersecting itself in time.

All interiors are the same one, intersecting itself in space.

A room is inside. Inside of what? A house. A house is inside. Inside of what? A world. A world is inside. Inside of what? A space. A space is inside. Inside of what? A universe.

All such chains end at the universe. It is the interior.

And it is also my interior. From this perspective, I understand why she hated mess.

From this perspective, I understand why I hated spotlessness.

It was an aesthetic war, a war of Aphrodite, of canon and great masters of organization.

To have a messy room is to have a messy interior.

And this is taken to be a sign that there are somehow no more questions worth asking.

It is an artifact of property, of having properties, as though people have constants and are not made of variables.

As though heaven chose an administrator out of sufficient immutability.

As though to have a messy room is to lay waste to the universe.

I don't mean to lay waste to the universe, mom. I just forget things have endings.

Or at least, I end up assuming they aren't my own to deal with.

I have a problem with the implicit structural rule of weakening.

Even having learned the secrets of linear logic.

I was raised by intuitionism, and facts, and patterns.

I have the worst case of universal endometriosis.
