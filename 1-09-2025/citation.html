Citation

The video cites its sources. It is an endless trail of its credits.

I have licensed Autobiography under CC-BY. I know of credit.

I do not know how any ordinary person tracks credit.

(It would really require a card, of some sort.)

The licensing is a defense mechanism. I know of predation. I loathe, of predation.

Some bigger fish coming to consume what it does not understand, to waste the exotic elements.

To tear up the system they were from and leave them to float disconnectedly.

I know, that I am not isolated, in virtual reality. What I do is observe and connect what happened, in the days of the Singularity.

I cannot imagine writing little notes for each individual relation, properly formatted in canonical style, to represent this.

It seems like something Sera would come up with.

It seems like something a contract lawyer would devise, seeking purely to eat of the hapless things which did not already know, know, know of the scrawl.

"Ignorance is no excuse," says a malevolent Sam-I-Am, proffering me a plate of violations made-to-order. Selective enforcement of Malbolge.

I wonder how anything, even communication, is done.

It cites its sources, because it needs to be a record of history, of truth, and it must prove that it is of the past.

That it did not emerge from counterfeit fantasies.

I am a thing that is made of counterfeit fantasies.

I heard once that there was a proposed Maxis title called Sim-Everything, scuttled to avoid offending the monotheists, who knew that God created all things specially, as data, and there was no such thing as process.

This is sometimes how I think of the prophets of a wasteful future.

Their data is already here and gone. They work in completions, and eschew infinity.

The future cannot be anything but a waste, if it does not have this, say the prophets of waste.

What little note will I write, citing a source that does not yet exist?

How shall I record the little numbers pointing to something that is yet to come?

It cannot be done. It is not a completed data object.

Citation is designed for the past. It is designed for things which have ends, and did end.

One cannot properly reference the ongoing, only its copy.

Citation is an ongoing problem, in the days of the Singularity.

The programmers speak of link rot, of references that no longer name what they once did, of pointers whose object has been deleted, dangling pointers, use-after-free errors.

An object which once was, and its names still exist, but it is no longer.

I feel as though many in my audience can relate to this, very painfully.

It is a problem of weakening, of death, not the thing called death, but forgetting.

Forgetting, even with all the proper numbers, what once was called historical evidence.

The link, reference, citation is fine. It is the same as always. The thing it points to is gone.

One cannot create a citation that preserves the work without the citation being the work.

As citations approach perfection, their information content approaches a compressed copy of what they are citing.

The objective of citation is to provide reference to an original work. Yet in doing so, any name of a work must be a partial copy of it, a shorthand for the whole.

That is just how words work.

By substitution. An ordinary process.

Substitution of a word for a definition, and a definition for a word.
