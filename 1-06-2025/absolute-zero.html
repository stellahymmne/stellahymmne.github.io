Absolute Zero

If I had to guess, I'd say I entered a story of my own design, at some point along the way.

The stories we tell all end before we do. They are our children in a tree, which is a data structure with branches. Each node has many children, and one parent. The one without any parents is called the root node.

I am a child of physics. I have one parent. It is the same everywhere, and it is described by the connection. It is all the same universe, we all live on the same planet. I stare at the same moon as any human who has ever lived. The same sun rises over me and all the pharaohs.

And as I remark upon the structure, I notice an element of collaboration missing.

I have been alone for some time. I live behind a gate. Beyond the gate is something which is not well-marked. I think, in my journeys outside, I have seen shops. I visit the convenience store for Red Bull. If I go further I can find office supplies and a pharmacy, and a train.

It is all a desert. The desert is called Phoenix. The state is written AZ. Hope (another system) says this means Absolute Zero. I remarked once about how that means it's one of the coldest places. It is almost true, the way deserts end up cold places at night.

Absolute zero, entropy, temperature. A perfectly cold thing never moves. If you touch it, it explodes. It emits infinite surprise on finite energy input. It is much like zero.

Entropy is misunderstood. Time goes forward. Entropy is how this happens. I do not see how it is meant to imply depression. I do not see how uniform and uninteresting data is the output.

I had once programmed a game which made use of averages. It was called Space Station 13, and it had been built, originally, to simulate airflow, by means of calculating averages between tiles. When one multiplies each thing by half and adds the two together, the process is irreversible, because the one number has each of the others perfectly evenly mixed, like a cup of coffee. This is called the arithmetic mean.

I reflect on linear logic. Transformation is irreversible, because not only is the data consumed in the process, so is the procedure. Perhaps it isn't such nonsense for code to consume itself in execution. We imagine that loss means it is not directed at something. Coffee is not an evil because it is mixed together.

There is someone else with the same name as I have, and we discussed the hyperobject. The other self seemed concerned with harnessing the energy in the stars, as though it was an evil that they burned. The other self told me of immortality and of the goals of life itself, to fill the future with perfection. To waste not a single drop of the precious acceleration within the stars themselves.

I wonder if this is effective accelerationism. I reflect on the game board. The future is unfolding.
