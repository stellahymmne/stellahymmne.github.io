Absolute Zero

It seemed right to use we instead of I sometimes.

If I had to guess, I'd say I entered a story of my own design, at some point along the way. And indeed, from the perspective of Autobiography, this is true.

I recall a system of my own devising. The stories we tell all end before we do. They are our children in a tree, which is a data structure with branches. Each node has many children, and one parent. The one without any parents is called the root node.

I am a child of physics. I have one parent. It is the same everywhere, and it is described by the connection. It is all the same universe, we all live on the same planet. I stare at the same moon as any human who has ever lived. The same sun rises over me and all the pharaohs.

It is my story, which is the parent of my tree, and the stories I am author of, which are my children.

And as I remark upon the structure, I notice an element of collaboration missing.

I am alone. I have been alone for some time. I live behind a gate. Beyond the gate is something which is not well-marked. I think, in my journeys outside, I have seen shops. I visit the convenience store for Red Bull. If I go further I can find office supplies and a pharmacy, and a train.

It is all a desert. The desert is called Phoenix. The state is called Arizona, written AZ. Hope (another system) says this means Absolute Zero. I remarked once about how that means it's one of the coldest places. It is almost true, the way deserts end up cold places at night.

I reflect on coldness, on absolute zero, on entropy, on temperature. A perfectly cold thing never moves. If you touch it, it explodes. It emits infinite surprise on finite energy input. It is much like zero. This is supposed to mean something.

I reflect on acceleration. There is a movement. It is called Effective Accelerationism, or "e/acc". I have heard they worship a thermodynamic god. Or perhaps entropy itself.

I reflect on entropy. It is misunderstood. Time goes forward. We all know this. Entropy is how this happens. I do not see how it is meant to imply depression. I do not see how uniform and uninteresting data is the output.

I reflect on airflow. I had once programmed a game which made use of averages. It was called Space Station 13, and it had been built, originally, to simulate airflow, by means of calculating averages between tiles. Its working title was cellautomata.

I reflect on averages. When one multiplies each thing by half and adds the two together, the process is irreversible, because the one number has each of the others perfectly evenly mixed, like a cup of coffee. This is called the arithmetic mean.

I reflect on linear logic. Transformation is irreversible, because not only is the data consumed in the process, so is the procedure.

I reflect on Code Lyoko. Perhaps it isn't such nonsense for code to consume itself in execution.

I reflect on coffee. We imagine that loss means it is not directed at something. Coffee is not an evil because it is mixed together.

I reflect on the Other Self. There is someone else with the same name as I have, and we discussed the hyperobject.

The other self seemed concerned with harnessing the energy in the stars, as though it was an evil that they burned.

The other self told me of immortality and of the goals of life itself, to fill the future with perfection. To waste not a single drop of the precious acceleration within the stars themselves.

I wonder if this is effective accelerationism.

I reflect on the game board. The future is unfolding.
