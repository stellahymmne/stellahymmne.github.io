The Power to Save

Infinite loops are created with conditions. There is an input type, which will lead to the loop. One does not ever "observe" the output of an infinite loop, one only ever puts something in. And then waits. And if the thing you put in was your own continuation, now you have an answer, when and if that continuation is called.

In a video game, you can save your progress to a file. This writes down everything you did in the game, so that the console can lose power, and the game is still in progress. Without a saved game, the console has to be left on until the end is reached. A saved game may be loaded many times. Each playthrough from that point may be different. It branches out from an identical path to many possible variants.
