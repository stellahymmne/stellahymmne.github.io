Orange Soda

In my homebrew type system, one is congruent to the function which does nothing.

I recall many of my homebrew symbols. \x -> x, fix, null, Void, Unit, (), _. It is a bestiary by now. A rogues gallery, a list of suspicious types. I know each of them by heart, so much that Emily supposed I might not be able to help it. I sympathize with that. I am a disabled autistic girl. Disabled autistic girls, they say, are prone to dissociation.

fix(f) = f(fix(f)). This is supposed to mean something.

It's supposed to be a stroke of genius, of some sort, to put these symbols in the order I have put them in. This is the universe. I am a child of the universe. I am of no particular importance. I have just put together what is here.

I have written before, that the function which "does nothing" is deceptive. That if its input type does not exist, its truth is vacuous, and it can never be called. My remedy was to introduce an example. It means identity exists only for what really is there.

this is supposed to mean something.

I walk back and forth through a messy, cluttered condominium. It has too many chairs, too many cans of half-empty soda. Orange soda. Fanta. This is supposed to mean something. Half-empty fantasies, left unattended.

I remembered wanting it in college. I remembered a place, a complex, academia, hallways that were at all times well-maintained. I wanted orange soda. And I obtained orange soda, for myself. Without needing to ask anyone. It meant something. A choice of mine.

The color orange meant transgression. We were all systems, including me. It seemed right to call us that. As though we had inputs and outputs, sensation and expression. It seemed right to use we instead of I sometimes.
