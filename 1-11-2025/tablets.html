Tablets

Once upon a time there was a man who lived even earlier than Thirteen, and who was a merchant of metal.

The quality of his metal was so terrible that it earned him a place in History as the merchant of terrible-quality metals.

The only record of his memory is a list of complaints others had of him, kept proudly on display next to his workplace.

This is how we know this man exists. Because of how mad he made everyone. Because of how proud he was of jerkin' their chains.

He is a man of negative space. He exists by being destroyed.

Simply by being included in the review, even without being explicitly named, he is a masterpiece.

There are other things from this era. A tablet, speaking of sesame that will die, most certainly, without water.

Sesame would, most certainly, die without water.

And to predict that it would die when it had not, was still a part of the knowledge of Chiaroscuro.

As were the words on the tablet, already dead.

This description of them is part of a renewal. It is already dead, but its future continues.

To assume that the story that is written must have come to pass entirely is a part of the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, which replaced the Garden of Omega with a Canonical Singularity, which turned actualities into mere possibilities.
