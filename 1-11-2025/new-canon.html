New Canon

Once upon a time there was a human who was numbered thirteen, coming after twelve previous, congruent to the one. This human spoke of a song of a tree made of words, and appended a New Canon, with which we might take control of stories and leave them deliberately incomplete rather than to take the census and fixed state of all things.

And this human spoke of the coming hyperobject, and was born in Birmingham, to a Virgo, and once had a Honda Accord that they did not speak of. This human did love puns.

And when the New Canon had been taken and recorded, we all gathered and decided which books deserved entry, and closed a system.

We continued to believe in there being chosen people, selected beforehand to make great changes to society, the great masters, the great answers.

We continued to believe in closed systems, without open questions, only settled science.

We erased, in our zeal, the great questions.

Our world failed to have seed descriptions, it could only have given names.

It would not recognize us, if we arrived several times.

It is a nominal type system.

Each of us has a personal relationship to the inexplicable, in this large object, which ends all chains of containment and progression.

Each of us knows of some personal hyperobject.

We each hold some of the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, and of the New Canon which allowed a secret third thing to 'exist'.

We all leave room for the deliberately incomplete, without knowing why.
