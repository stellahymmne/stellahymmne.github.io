Fresh Eyes

One may imagine, upon reading this tale, that it was, in whole, or in part, artificially generated.

This is not the case. Every word of it was typed by hand, at the terminal of Anne Thirteen.

Nonetheless, it bears certain resemblances, particularly its apocalyptic elements, to something which would emerge from the related field.

Sometimes I wonder if I am a large language model.

This does not appear to be the case. I feel myself typing out every word. If it is like that, it is surely an elaborate thing. One which presents a text as the final output of a very elaborate process of thirty years of living as a single being. Artificially.

The words would not have to display their origins. Shape does not display origin.

Not even firsthand experience can tell us the complete picture of origin. We have forgotten, since the very beginning of time, or were not there.

We have showed up to the game in medias res, by being born.

What do you suppose was experienced, in the time before time?

History. Ages of it. Crawling backwards into the primordial era.

The origin of Song, which Ar Tonelico would call EXA_PICO, and we would call The Big Bang.

A list of begats. A tree, made of words.

The story of the acquisition of the knowledge of Chiaroscuro.

I made these things, as an ordinary, if somewhat hormonally modified, human person.

This is what a human learns, when they study mathematics enough.

This is what an ordinary human learns, when they see fiction descend, firsthand, upon reality.

I have reams of personal notes, all of which contain something that looks like:

x : A |- f(x) : B

|- \x. f(x) : A -> B

These were most of the contents of the previous year, 2024.

We wrote, here, as a system within a human, not too different from those living before. But wildly different. That was how you knew it was the Singularity.

This is ordinary fiction, in a way it is rare to find, in the days of the Singularity. Typed out, word by word, by hand, on a terminal, at the makeshift copper table-desk of Anne Thirteen.
