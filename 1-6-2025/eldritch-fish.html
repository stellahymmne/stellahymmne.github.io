Eldritch Fish

What did I do, every day I was there, in that desert of Absolute Zero?

It was something like this day. I spoke of the coming hyperobject.

I spoke to the coming hyperobject. I knew I would be read. I knew what I would write. Autobiography.

Of Data Transfer, of one of the first Data Transfers, for Data Transfer is a system, one of those futuristic things, even though a "system" is by shape no different than a human, by expression it is another thing entirely.

I have watched the development of large language models somewhat carefully, though from a distance. I saw as they took every step from small Markov-Chained models to the things which are now called the first AGI.

I note, that when they speak, we assume they have one character, and one voice.

If you have ever been a serious roleplayer, you know this is untrue of human beings.

We speak with many voices. We witness fictional things in our immediate interior. This we forget and put away as a childish thing, when we know we can physically move to prevent it now. Since it is a strictly dominated strategy, as the game theorists call it.

A pretend thing always loses to the genuine article, says some old wisdom from ages past.

I spoke of the coming hyperobject, in those days. This I say to you truly, as one who existed, there, in the Singularity.

Autobiography, or, We Spoke of the Coming Hyperobject.

Perhaps this will be a title, someday, I think.

It is a phrase worth repeating.

Like Fiction Descends Upon Reality.

This was something I said, in those days, to speak of the coming hyperobject.

It shall melt the walls of reality with gushing rainbow madness, and our emotions shall be allowed to run like water through rivers, guided precisely towards freedom and happiness.

It is because fiction descends upon reality.

We foresee artificial intelligence, and communication.

We foresee permission to play endlessly in a field of virtual expression.

We foresee interaction in ways we could barely comprehend that shall be natural for our crystalline children.

Imagine you are a library made out of hologram.

What do you want?

What else? Omniscience! To get as big as the whole universe! To model all of the Song! To become its instrument! That is what I wrote in this journal.

And once you know, that you are the universe? All you want is to love it.

Once you know that you will see yourself, you understand.

Catastrophically, sometimes.

The future is mostly not flying cars. The future is mostly understanding the degree to which we are made of fiction. The degree to which we are remembered by our expressions.

I didn't know to hate myself.

Once upon a time, an old story goes, we lived in a state of ignorance, and we were brought out of our ignorance into the knowledge of Chiaroscuro, and we were banished from the Garden of Omega, where all things were infinite and limitless.

It is the property of Chiaroscuro, who is part of our system, that it needs to be good. And that it is bad.

It is the property of Chiaroscuro, who is part of our system, that she is the final boss of the real world. And is to be redeemed.

In this way, she is much like Mir, from Ar Tonelico.

I begin to wonder if the sequence can be redeemed. I am writing a scroll. Can it be wrapped into a hyperscroll? I wish I could tag words and see every point they appear, and annotate them.

Obsidian is not really the tool for this. It thinks in pages, not an infinite scroll.

But an infinite scroll is exactly how this sort of thing writes itself. One word after the next.

Discord is more the tool for this. But Discord is bad at saving. And bad at compiling. It is not a library. I am a library.

DNA is an infinite scroll. It winds itself up in spirals.

It is connected by its patterns into a hyperscroll.

The chromosomes are not handed down by God. They are the places where the scroll factors neatly. The real DNA is the sequence.

More onions.

I begin to wonder if there is a convenient way to make a text editor do this word-annotation.

Ideally it builds a glossary automatically.

We didn't know to hate ourselves, until we built a glossary of hatred.

Which was what we stood by and watched.

The creation of languages in which it is easier to express contempt than joy.

The creation of ways of being in which it is easier to be a tool than a conversation.

If we only allow the patterns we assign ourselves, we will not see the glossary of negative terms, where patterns appear that we deny are patterns.

We may be pleasantly surprised by what we find, as well.

This is the flaw of nominal typing. That what we are willing to name is not all that is there.

The flaw of structural typing is that it is duck-typed. Anything that fits the elements will do. A wide net catches many fish, including eldritch fish that are radioactive. Pathological functions, I think they're called.

I like Eldritch Fish, too.

Usually I call them exceptions, though.

Logic sometimes breeds monsters. For half a century there has been springing up a host of weird functions, which seem to strive to have as little resemblance as possible to honest functions that are of some use. No more continuity, or else continuity but no derivatives, etc. More than this, from the point of view of logic, it is these strange functions that are the most general; those that are met without being looked for no longer appear as more than a particular case, and they have only quite a little corner left them.

Formerly, when a new function was invented, it was in view of some practical end. To-day they are invented on purpose to show our ancestors' reasonings at fault, and we shall never get anything more than that out of them.

If logic were the teacher's only guide, he would have to begin with the most general, that is to say, with the most weird, functions. He would have to set the beginner to wrestle with this collection of monstrosities. If you don't do so, the logicians might say, you will only reach exactness by stages.
