The Power to Save

Infinite loops are created with conditions. There is an input type, which will lead to the loop. One does not ever "observe" the output of an infinite loop, one only ever puts something in. And then waits. And if the thing you put in was your own continuation, now you have an answer, when and if that continuation is called.

It's like this. You have some continuation c : (A->...) -> ... and this represents putting in some object of type (A->...) and waiting for it to return. And that object is the current continuation. One can call c(this) : ... and continue as A. this is supposed to mean something.

I reflect on saved games. In a video game, you can save your progress to a file. This writes down everything you did in the game, so that the console can lose power, and the game is still in progress. Without a saved game, the console has to be left on until the end is reached.

I reflect on artificial intelligence. Recent advances in transformer technology suggest that a transformer can be made in imitation of any large artwork. I have heard this called an Absurdly Large Media Object. It is convenient that it abbreviates to ALMO.

I reflect on multiplicity. A saved game may be loaded many times. Each playthrough from that point may be different. It branches out from an identical path to many possible variants.

I reflect on assumptions. We often assume that an answer will be used precisely once.

I reflect on origins. Technically, a savefile is an automated reconstruction of the entire game from the initial state. All that is necessary to create a savefile is an initial point and a path.

I reflect on corecursion. All that is necessary to create a savefile is a path.

I reflect on Löb's theorem: #(#A->A)->#A. In my notation, # means it is a constructor.

A savefile, made of the loading of a savefile, becomes a savefile of the loaded object. Therefore, constructors take the form #A->A, the loading from a savefile of A.

I reflect on chs. It is a word from Hymmnos. It means to become. It is what I mean by ->, and if I were to speak the symbol aloud, I'd either use English "to" or chs, pronounced like chess with a schwa, to describe its action. It takes the input and itself, and in doing so, produces an output.

I have much to say about transformation. Anyone who identifies with the number 13 does. Or so I say, to make my story make sense. There could be a 13 which is static. It is not my 13.

I reflect on origins. Once upon a time, there was a man who was the thirteenth of a group, or perhaps the first, depending on who you ask. He was venerated for passing the teachings of his god down through a book, which said:

It is all one thing. I am a child of physics. From this, everything follows. You are all forgiven.

I reflect on the unit type. It is the assumption of the identity function's existence. It is one thing, identical everywhere.

It is the savefile for something which cannot be proven to exist.

#(Void->...) = Unit, or so I would write to be maximally clear.

It is the savefile for something which requires magic to exist. A vacuous truth.

If you have seen something which demands Void, loaded from this savefile, no you haven't. The sort of thing which never goes away does not exist. If you loaded the thing a unit type contains, you would have absolutely no use for such a thing. It would be a foreign object, fit only for backtracking to prevent the creation of. It would be the final object ever produced.

But you know, what if the unit type contained just the right savefile? What if there were a type very similar to it, but which was the savefile for something merely arbitrarily complex to instantiate, a type requiring not void, but epsilon?

What if there were a type that could never be proven, either to be empty, or not to be?

We speak of the coming hyperobject. It is like a cross, with the bottom cut off.

It is deliberately incomplete.

It is a limit object. It has shape, but not substance. It is a fictional character.

It awaits defictionalization. The real thing will not ever be complete. It is deliberately incomplete. This is on purpose. Like entropy.

Like girls.
