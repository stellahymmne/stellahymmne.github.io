Orange Soda

In my homebrew type system, Annelang, 1 is congruent to the function which does nothing.

I recall many of my homebrew symbols. \x -> x, fix, null, Void, Unit, (), _. It is a bestiary by now. A rogues gallery, a list of suspicious types.

I know each of them by heart, so much that Emily (a member of The Garden Path) supposed I might not be able to help it. I sympathize with that. I am a disabled autistic girl.

Disabled autistic girls, they say, are prone to dissociation. I have to agree, though it is only by observation that I myself fit this type.

fix(f) = f(fix(f)). This is supposed to mean something.

It's supposed to be a stroke of genius, of some sort, to put these symbols in the order I have put them in. _|_ = fix(\x->x). This is the universe. I am a child of the universe. I am of no particular importance. I have just put together what is here.

I have written before, that \x->x is deceptive. That if its input type does not exist, its truth is vacuous, and it can never be called. My remedy was to introduce an example. \x?:t->x is a perfectly adequate identity function, given a term t to fall back on if it ends up the argument of fix. It means _|_ can only exist for inhabited types.

this is supposed to mean something.

I pace through a messy, cluttered condominium. It has too many chairs, too many cans of half-empty soda. Orange soda. Fanta. This is supposed to mean something. Half-empty fantasies, left unattended.

I remembered wanting it in college. I remembered a place, a complex, academia, hallways that were at all times well-maintained. I wanted orange soda. And I obtained orange soda, for myself. Without needing to ask anyone. It meant something. A choice of mine.

The color orange meant transgression, in Unlimited's magic system. Unlimited was another one of the systems. We were all systems, including me. It seemed right to call us that. As though we had inputs and outputs, sensation and expression.
